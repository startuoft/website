Space Travel Analog Research Team (START)

The Team


START is a team of University of Toronto students participating in the 2024/2025 Canadian Stratospheric Balloon Experiment Design Challenge.

This competition allows university students to design and test a small scientific experiment on a high-altitude balloon provided by the Canadian Space Agency.

START also provides opportunities for undergraduate students to expand their professional network and develop technical skills which build strong and confident future engineers, scientists and researchers.

This Year’s Mission


As NASA’s Gateway project works to return humanity to the moon and new engineering solutions are crucial for success. Without Earth’s atmosphere to protect astronauts, space radiation becomes a major threat to their health and future space exploration.

Fortunately, the stratospheric environment serves as an ideal Earth-based analog to space radiation. Our design aims to expose human cells to this radiation while keeping them alive, offering a unique opportunity for research on the impact that Galactic Cosmic Rays have on human DNA.

Our Payload


This payload is designed to support research on the biological effects of Galactic Cosmic Rays (GCRs) by exposing human cell cultures to the stratospheric environment.

The payload consists of multiple subsystems that ensure the biological samples remain within controlled conditions throughout the flight. These include a temperature-controlled chamber, a pressurized containment system, and a robust structural enclosure to protect against external environmental factors.

Community Outreach


We had the privilege to host a four hour workshop during the National Society of Black Engineers’ Annual High School Conference.

We gave a thirty minute lecture on galactic cosmic rays, the stratospheric environment and relevant material properties.
